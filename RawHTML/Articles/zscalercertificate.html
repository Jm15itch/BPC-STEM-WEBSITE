How to install Zscaler certificates on Linux

Article written by Jack Mitchell (5/6/2023)

Zscaler is a firewall commonly used in educational or professional workplaces to block access inappropriate or malicious content. Zscaler uses a certificate to decrypt traffic from your device so it can inspect where it's going and what you're doing, this is generated uniquely. If you plan to use Linux while also having access to the internet then you will have to follow this tutorial.

Step 1. Find your certificate

If your administrator is friendly to Linux then you may be able to ask for it, however, if they aren't then you'll have to look for one. A common place to look is your workplace's Google Drive, go to your Drive and search "Zscaler" or "Certificate". You are looking for a certificate file that is named something like, "ZscalerRootCertificate-2048-SHA256.crt".

Step 2. Install

These are the steps required for their respective distributions.

Arch / EndeavourOS / Garuda

sudo trust anchor --store ZscalerRootCertificate-2048-SHA256.crt

Debian / Ubuntu / Mint

sudo cp ZscalerRootCertificate-2048-SHA256.crt /usr/local/share/ca-certificates/ZscalerRootCertificate.crt; sudo update-ca-certificates

Step 3. Connect

If everything went ok, then you should be able to connect to your workplaces wifi using PEAP authentication and your username and password. If anything went wrong, I left out your distribution, or you need further help then feel free to contact me and I'll happily guide you through the process.
